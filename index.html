Project Overview

Sigma.Net is an internet provider, currently facing a serious business challenge: a customer churn rate of 57.86%, which exceeds the industry norm of 20–40% and directly impacts retention and profitability. Despite offering a structured service process—from marketing and onboarding to activation, support, and billing—gaps in early customer experience, pricing, and service reliability lead to early disengagement. The project's core objective is to reduce churn to 45% within three months by uncovering root causes through data-driven analysis and delivering actionable improvements.

Methodology

  1. Business Understanding
    Gained a clear understanding of the business challenge through data exploration and industry research, then defined the project objectives.
  2. Data Preparation & Cleaning
    Prepared and cleaned the dataset using Python to ensure accuracy and consistency.
  3. Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)
    Analyzed customer behavior and demographic patterns to uncover churn patterns, using Python and Power BI.
  4. Predictive Analysis
    Applied a decision tree algorithm to evaluate variable significance and build a churn prediction model.
  5. Data Visualization
    Created relevant charts with Python and Power BI, then developed a comprehensive dashboard in Power BI.
  6. Insight & Recommendation
    Generated actionable insights and formulated recommendations based on analysis results to reduce churn and improve customer retention.

Insight

Churn is Driven by Poor Early Experience
The primary reason for Sigma.Net’s high churn rate of 57.86% is customers leaving early, typically within just a few months, due to disappointing first impressions. This is often tied to uncompetitive pricing, unreliable internet service, and overly restrictive download limits.

New Customers Are the Most Vulnerable
Around 71% of churning customers are relatively new, suggesting that Sigma.Net's onboarding process fails to meet expectations or deliver immediate value.

Cost Sensitivity is High
Customers facing monthly bills higher than $18 are more likely to churn, especially when the perceived value doesn't justify the cost. The sweet spot for retention lies between $6 and $18 per month.

Service Quality Impacts Loyalty
While not the leading cause, frequent service failures such as disconnections or technical issues contribute meaningfully to churn, especially among customers who've already experienced other friction points.

Download Limits Cause Frustration
Customers who repeatedly breach download limits are at a significantly higher risk of churning. This points to a need for more flexible or generous data plans.

Internet-Only Plans May Underperform
Customers who subscribe only to internet packages are disproportionately represented among churned users, highlighting possible dissatisfaction with stand-alone service quality or pricing.

Recommendation

  1. Improve First Impressions for New Customers
    • Offer more competitive and flexible pricing plans to enhance perceived value.
    • Focus onboarding efforts on delivering a positive early experience.
  2. Revise Pricing Strategy
    • Introduce tiered plans that match various customer needs.
    • Ensure pricing aligns with customer expectations and industry standards.
    • Expected impact: ~8% retention improvement.
  3. Increase Download Limits
    • Raise the overall download limit threshold to reduce customer frustration.
    • Offer flexible add-on packages for high-usage customers instead of strict penalties.
    • Expected impact: ~3% retention improvement.
  4. Enhance Service Quality and Infrastructure
    • Improve customer support responsiveness to reduce churn-related friction.
    • Invest in network upgrades to minimize service disruptions and outages.
    • Expected impact: ~5% retention improvement.
  5. Use Predictive AI for Proactive Intervention
    • Deploy a Random Forest churn prediction model to predict customer churn.
    • Integrate model results into a dashboard that highlights high-risk users.
    • Enable proactive retention actions tailored to predicted churn behavior.
